NIKOLA TESLA



Nikola Tesla was a pioneering inventor and electrical engineer who lived from 1856 to 1943. He was born in Serbia and later became a naturalized American citizen. Tesla is best known for his contributions to the development of alternating current (AC) electrical systems, which revolutionized the transmission and distribution of electricity. His inventions and discoveries also include the Tesla coil, which is still used in radio technology today, and early work on wireless communication and electricity transmission.

Despite his brilliance, Tesla faced numerous challenges throughout his career, including financial difficulties and disputes with other inventors like Thomas Edison. He was known for his eccentricities and visionary ideas, such as wireless power transmission and the development of a "world system" of energy distribution.

Tesla's legacy extends beyond his inventions; he left a lasting impact on science and technology, inspiring generations of inventors and engineers. Today, he is revered as one of the greatest minds of the electrical age, with his name immortalized in the unit of magnetic flux density, the "tesla."
